The Battle of Quebec: September 13,1759

This is a hand colored original print of the Battle of Quebec. It was first produced and published in the 1760 edition of The "London" Magazine. It was purchased in 1957 before the restored Louis Bolduc House was opened to the public.

It needs some special attention. The print is not archivally framed so it is vulnerable to deterioration caused by fluctuations in temperature, humidity, and light. It will cost around $350 to address the preservation needs of this wonderful print.

The English and the French are fighting here. The English won the battle.

Here we meet some of the major players for the American Revolution in the French & Indian War - research George Washington, for starters.

As a result of this battle in the Seven Years War (as it is known in Europe (known as the French & Indian War in North America), Spain got the French territory west of the Mississippi River and New Orleans. Leaving Quebec

What is this?

  • A map of Canada and La Nouvelle France
  • An original 1755 document on parchment
  • A display of where the Native American tribes were located in 1755

How did we get this map?

It was purchased based on the information in the inventory of Louis Bolduc’s property after the death of his second wife, Agatha Govreau. The National Society of the Colonial Dames of America in the State of Missouri funded its acquisition in 1957

Does it need any special care or restoration?

  • It was archivally framed several years ago
  • There is some damage from where our interpreters used a pointer to locate Ste. Genevieve on the map
  • It is difficult for visitors to read easily because the Bolduc House Museum has an inadequate lighting system

What is important about the map?

  • It was drawn by D’Anville, the most prolific cartographer of Louis XV. He never left Paris so to draw the map D;Anville required really good technical communication skills from the explorers.
  • The longitude notation uses a prime meridian different from Greenwich, England.
  • It shows Ste. Genevieve, Kaskaskia, and Cahokia but not St. Louis.
  • It is centered on Washington. D.C.

What inaccuracies are there on this map?

  • Lake Michigan should not be due north of the Florida peninsula.
  • Lake Michigan is too little.

Can you trace Louis Bolduc’s trip from Quebec to Ste. Genevieve on the map?

  • Find Chicago where Louis’ wife, Marguerite and his baby son, Louis both died (Oh, wait, it’s just a stinking field of onions in 1755)
  • Go down the Illinois River until you reach the Mississippi River—What is its other name? (Le Fleuve de St. Louis)
  • If you cross the Ohio River are you north or south of Ste. Genevieve? (south too far)
