Children's Activities

Do you want to know what it was like to live in Missouri during the eighteenth century? Thanks to seven year old Henry Brackenridge you can. He was sent to Ste. Genevieve in 1793 by his father in Philadelphia to learn French. He stayed for three years and returned when he was a grown-up. Then he wrote several books with detailed information about his time in Ste. Genevieve—playing with clay marbles, learning to shoot arrows with Shawnee Indian boys, learning to dance the minuet at children’s balls after Mass, and being really really sick. We’ll show you what his experience would have been like using his own words. See our Easy Reader tab for easy reading.

Regarding Our Heroic Squirrel Mascot, Zuts

Find the footprints made by Zuts, our virtual mascot!

He is a grey squirrel descended from ‘Tit Monsieur, the secret pet of Agatha Bolduc who learned to read and write and started a journal of his observations of the humans in the Bolduc House.

In the museum, every artifact marked with one of Zuts’ footprints can be touched.

Hi. My name is Henry Brack-en-ridge.

I am seven years old.

I am from Phil-a-del-phi-a.

That is far away from here!

In 1793 my father sent me here to learn French.

Lots of people here speak French.

I stayed here three years.

Later I wrote books about this.

I play with clay marbles. These are balls.

Shawnee Indian boys are my friends. They teach me to shoot arrows.

I am learning to dance the min-u-et. We dance after church on Sunday.

Sometimes I get really, really sick.

I don't like to be sick.

I like to run and play with my friends.

Note: These are not Henry's words. This is for our young readers.
