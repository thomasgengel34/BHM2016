Where

Typically everyone gathers at the Linden House and then goes wherever. All tours start there. All classes meet there. Please check something besides the Linden House ONLY if you KNOW you are supposed to meet elsewhere. That way, if you are wondering where your group is, we can find you!

Linden House
J.B. Valle House
LeMeilleur House

Those are the ONLY places you should ever start, unless someone on our staff has told you otherwise.

How

There is a map on the About page, as well as links to maps you can find on the internet for directions.

Ste. Genevieve has MANY restaurants, hotels, bread-and-breakfasts, etc., if you are coming from out of town.

Wear comfortable shoes. Think happy thoughts. Bring a smile, just in case you need it.

Why

These are reflective questions for you. Giving us your answers might help us make your visit more satisifying. Why are you coming? What do you want to know? What do you want to do? See? What, specifically, would tell you that your visit was successful (for the goal oriented) or enjoyable (for the sanguine)? Don't feel like you need to give us answers.

I am bringing...

You might think about bringing a camera, project supplies, tools, a sketch pad. That might make a difference. For example, if you want to set up an easel and paint, we would like to know about it so we can coordinate our efforts. Members of tour groups might want to stop and give you, um, helpful suggestions. Or a wedding might start happening around you! And we'd like to know who is about the place.

Dress for the weather

We have seasons. Oh, do we have seasons! We are on the Mississippi River and we have a Midwestern climate. Expect hot humidity in summer and snow and ice in winter.And some of our buildings are neither heated or air-conditioned. So you might want to check!

I Want to Volunteer...

This email will go to participate@bolduchouse.org

Are you interested in volunteering at the Bolduc House Museum?

We have a wide array of activities and departments that we are always looking for volunteers for from caring for our gardens, to help in the Archives, or helping with Historical Activities.

Please tell us what you are interested in helping with. For example (This is NOT a complete list):

  • Gardening
  • Landscaping
  • Handyman
  • Furniture Refinishing
  • Library / Archives
  • Costume Making
  • Tailoring / fur-making
  • Carpentry
  • Moving furniture and other heavy stuff
  • Flatwork
  • Tuckpointing and masonry
  • Exterior Painting
  • Interior Painting
  • Translating French to English
  • Warp Drive technology
  • Weaving
  • Candlemaking
  • Glass-blowing
  • Blacksmithing / coppersmithing / metalworking
  • Seasonal decorating
  • Historical research
  • Preservation of artifacts
  • Cataloging
  • Office help
  • Period music
  • Story telling
  • Teaching period dance
  • Leading and teaching old-time games
  • Leading Tours
  • Historical acting / portrayal

In your email to us...

Please tell us if you have no experience, you are a beginner, a professional or an expert at what you are interested in.

What kind of a commitment in hours are you willing to make over the next six months?

How far from the Museum do you live? The Museum cannot furnish transportation. Can you furnish your own?

What does the Museum means to you?

Some skills need a lot of equipment, for example blacksmithing, that we do not have. If you are volunteering yours, please let us know.

Are you conversationally fluent in French? English? No? Well, how about Jawa? Klingon maybe? Elvish? Some of our visitors are from very far away. Far, far away. We never know who will come and it is good to be prepared.
